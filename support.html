Frequently Asked Questions

Where is my data stored?
All your financial data is stored locally on your device using Apple's SwiftData framework. It never leaves your device unless you choose to create a manual backup.

How do I backup my data?
You can create manual backups to your iCloud account through the settings menu in the app. This ensures you can restore your data if you switch devices.

Is my data secure?
Yes. We leverage iOS built-in encryption and offer optional biometric (Face ID/Touch ID) protection to keep your financial information private.

Adding Transactions with Siri

Mobile Ledger 1.7 added voice and Shortcuts support, so you can log expenses and income without opening the app.

Siri voice
Say "Hey Siri, add expense to Mobile Ledger" (or "Add income to Mobile Ledger"). Siri will ask for the amount, then which account, then the optional merchant and category.

Shortcuts app
Open the Shortcuts app, tap +, search "Mobile Ledger", and choose Add Expense or Add Income. Fill the fields, then run the action.

Spotlight
Swipe down on the Home Screen, type "add expense", and tap the action that appears.

Tip: voice transactions run entirely on-device — no internet, no servers, no audio ever leaves your phone.

Backup & Restore

Mobile Ledger creates encrypted ZIP backups in your iCloud Drive so you can move to a new device or recover from accidents.

Automatic backups
Settings → Sync → Manage Backups. Toggle "Automatic Backups" on. Backups run silently in the background while your phone is charging.

Create a backup now
Settings → Sync → Manage Backups → "Create Backup Now". The zip lands in iCloud Drive under Mobile Ledger.

Restore on another device
Open Mobile Ledger on the new device. Accept the iCloud backup prompt on first launch, or go to Settings → Sync → Manage Backups, tap any backup, and choose Restore.

Tip: backups include accounts, transactions, budgets, recurring rules, and your profile picture. Each device keeps its own retention window, so backups from your other devices stay visible too.

Setting Up Budgets

Budgets help you stay on track. Each budget is tied to a specific ledger and category, with optional threshold alerts.

Create a budget
Open the Budget tab, tap a category, and set the monthly amount. Repeat for each category you want to track.

Get alerts before you overspend
Settings → Notifications → Budget Threshold (Pro). Choose 80%, 90%, or 100% to receive a notification when you cross that mark.

Personal vs Business
Each ledger has its own budgets. Switch ledgers via the ledger menu at the top of the Budget tab — numbers stay scoped to whichever ledger is active.

Tip: Mobile Ledger 1.7 fixed a bug where a budget's percentage could exceed 100% from spending in other ledgers. Each ledger now keeps its own books.

Recurring Transactions

For bills, salaries, or subscriptions that repeat. Mobile Ledger can either auto-post them on the day they're due, or remind you to confirm.

Create a recurring rule
Transactions tab → Recurring → +. Set the amount, account, category, frequency (weekly, monthly, etc.), and start date.

Choose the mode
"Auto-post" inserts the transaction automatically on the due date. "Reminder" sends a notification asking you to confirm before posting.

Stop or change a rule
Tap a rule in the Recurring list to edit, pause, or delete it. Past auto-posted transactions are kept; only future scheduling changes.

Tip: recurring rules sync across iPhone, iPad, and Mac when iCloud sync is on, so you only set them up once.
